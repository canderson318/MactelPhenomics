The interpretation of the two identified trajectories requires further validation and contextualization. The manuscript states one trajectory is characterized by vascular alterations and the other by neurodegeneration, but recent studies suggest vascular changes may emerge later in MacTel progression rather than being a distinct early trajectory. The authors should compare their findings with existing MacTel classification systems (e.g., Chew et al.'s severity scale) and imaging-based progression patterns (e.g., ellipsoid zone loss trajectories) to clarify inconsistencies and confirm the novelty of their trajectories. Moreover, the link between trajectory assignment and clinical outcomes (e.g., macular neovascularization, vision loss progression) should be strengthened, as this would enhance the clinical relevance of the findings.
